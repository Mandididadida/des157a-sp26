Mad Libs Design V1

This project explores a playful first-person shopping story about a strange creature, unexpected fruit, and a very questionable pet choice.

Project Direction

For this version, I am developing a humorous Mad Libs story told in the first person. The tone is whimsical, strange, and slightly chaotic. I want the final experience to feel lighthearted and easy to understand, while still having a strong visual identity.

At this stage, I am focusing on the sketch, digital comp, layout planning, color direction, and how the story could be presented clearly on screen.

Story Draft

As I walked in, I saw another ________ (ANIMAL) named ________ (SAME FRIEND NAME) trying to buy a ________ (ANIMAL). I thought, “wow, that’s pretty ________ (ADJECTIVE).”

I kept walking until I found a pile of ________ (FRUIT). I picked one up and imagined taking it home, washing the outside with ________ (LIQUID), and then ________ (VERB) it while sitting on a/an ________ (OBJECT), eating it.

After thinking very ________ (ADVERB), I decided I also needed a pet. So I chose a/an ________ (ADJECTIVE) ________ (ANIMAL) that could ________ (VERB) and sometimes ________ (VERB). We will live together in ________ (PLACE) together.

Finally, I went to pay. The cashier looked at me very ________ (ADVERB). I reached into my ________ (CLOTHES) and pulled out ________ (TYPE OF MONEY). I paid with ________ (SAME TYPE OF MONEY), even though it was a little ________ (ADJECTIVE).

In the end, I left the ________ (STORE) feeling ________ (EMOTION), holding my ________ (FRUIT) and my new pet, ready for a/an ________ (ADJECTIVE) life.

Sketch & Digital Comp

The sketch explores the structure of the form view and the result view. The digital comp shows my early decisions for type, color, graphic elements, and layout.

Visual Notes

I want the final design to feel warm, playful, and a little absurd. I am interested in using rounded forms, cheerful colors, soft shadows, and illustrated shopping-related details to support the story.

My goal is to make the page engaging without making it feel crowded or hard to read.
